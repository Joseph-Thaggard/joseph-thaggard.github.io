Background

Who I Am

I'm a Junior Aerospace Engineering student at UAH, embedded in the Charger Advanced Power and Propulsion (CAPP) Lab since January 2023. My work sits at the intersection of plasma physics, electric propulsion, and the numerical simulations that make advanced concepts tractable.

I built a Farnsworth-type electrostatic fusion reactor from scratch — chamber, vacuum system, high-voltage integration, DAQ software, and all. I've qualified as an operator on the CAPP Lab's 60 kJ "Sparky" pulse power machine, and I lead simulation efforts on the OUROBOROS magnetically shielded Hall thruster project, including a second-author AIAA SciTech 2026 paper. I also conduct contracted PIC simulation research on magnetic mirror fusion confinement using WarpX, modeling DT plasma behavior across Gas Dynamic Trap reactor geometries.

Beyond the lab, I co-founded the Student Electric Propulsion Consortium (SEPC) in September 2025 — a worldwide student network now spanning 19+ universities — to provide student EP teams with resources to assist in concept development, connecting them to experts in industry and academia. I also serve as Chapter Chair of UAH's AIAA branch and sit on the AIAA Nuclear & Future Flight Propulsion Technical Committee.

Simulation and design lead for a 1.35 kW magnetically shielded Hall-effect thruster. Developed automated pyFEMM magnetic circuit models, benchmarked against SPT-100 using HallThruster.jl, and initiated 2D axial-azimuthal WarpX PIC discharge channel modeling. Second-author at AIAA SciTech 2026.

Fusion · Plasma Physics
Farnsworth Electrostatic Fusion Reactor
Designed, constructed, and operated a complete Farnsworth-type inertial electrostatic confinement reactor — including high-vacuum system (<0.32 mTorr), high-voltage integration, scintillation diagnostics, and full computer-controlled data acquisition using custom Arduino-based hardware and software.
High Vacuum Arduino DAQ Scintillation Detect. HV Systems
Software · Automation
FusorOS
A comprehensive plasma control software suite for autonomous reactor operation. Features a visual block-diagram programming interface (FusorProgram), an execution engine for generated command sequences (FusorAutoRun), real-time data visualization, and hardware-free test mode.

Fusion · Computational Physics
Magnetic Mirror Fusion Modeling
Contracted PIC simulation research modeling DT plasma confinement in ELMO Bumpy Torus and Gas Dynamic Trap (GDT) geometries using WarpX. Developed a spline-axis approach for simulating curved multi-mirror reactor configurations, increasing simulation simplicty. Implements Pairwise Coulomb, DSMC, fusion, and bremsstrahlung collision physics with deuterium-tritium plasma species.
WarpX / AMReX PIC + MCC DT Plasma ELMO / GDT Python RZ Geometry
